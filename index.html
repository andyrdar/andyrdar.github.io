"Understanding human interactions & how we perceive the world."

My identity as a researcher

My research as an early-career neuroscientist is driven by a fascination to understand how cognition and visual perception are shaped by interactions between neural circuits, specifically by how electrophysiological signals relating to cognitive processes can guide future neurotechnologies.

My identity as a science communicator

Rooted in social and cultural anthropology, a major goal of mine is to empower and create a support network for underrepresented students. Follow me on my journey to foster diversity inside and outside of science!

My identity as a human

I love humanity. How we build metaphors and romanticize our existence because we are able to recognize how beautiful it actually is that we are alive right this instant. But mainly, love, and everything that comprises it. Which really, is everything that exists. Understanding people and loving them in the process, caring and seeing and existing and listening and sharing, all of it.
