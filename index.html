Andrea as a researcher

My research as an early-career neuroscientist is driven by a fascination to understand how cognition and visual perception are shaped by interactions between neural circuits, specifically by how electrophysiological signals relating to cognitive processes can informhow these interactions occur at a circuit-level.

Andrea as a science communicator

Rooted in social and cultural anthropology, a major goal of mine is to empower and create a support network for underrepresented students. My path as a scientist has been greatly enriched by shared experiences with individuals who find motivation from uplifting others. Being in an environment surrounded by people who, like me, are familiar with the challenges that come with being an underrepresented student researcher, motivated me to play an active role in fostering diversity within academia.

Andrea as a human

Passion is such a strong word to me. When do you feel warmth? What ignites your drive? There are some simple things that come to mind that merge hobbies and likes, they could be subjectively called passions: flowers, smiles, cooking. But I also feel like there's this deeper feeling, more closely related to what can be copmmonly defined as passion, a constant in both what drives my actions and feels grander than myself. I love humanity.

How we build metaphors and romanticize our existence because we are able to recognize how beautiful it actually is that we are alive right this instant. But mainly, love, and everything that comprises it. Which really, is everything that exists. How do you choose to love? When do you feel the most loved? When was the first time you truly felt loved? Understanding people and loving them in the process, caring and seeing and existing and listening and sharing, all of it.
